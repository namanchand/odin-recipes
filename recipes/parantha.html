How to make parantha

Description

Parantha is an Indian dish which is made of wheat flour or and a filling of your choice It is very tasty and healthy.

It is mostly eaten in breakfast in northern side of India. Many people love to eat it with either butter or curt or chutney or achaar which magnifies the rich taste of this Indian dish.

Ingredients

  • For Dough

    • wheat flour or Maida flour: 250g
    • Oil: 2 teaspoon to knead
    • Water for kneading: 3/4 cup
    • Salt: 1/4 teaspoon or as per taste
    • Ghee for cooking

  • For Filling

    • Filling of your choice (200g): Potato/Paneer/Mooli/Gobhi/Onion/Mix Veg
    • Salt: As per taste
    • Green Chilies: 1-2 finely chopped (Optional)
    • Garlic: 2-3 cloves, minced (Optional)
    • Fresh coriander leaves: 2-3 tablespoons, chopped
    • Coriander powder: 1-2 teaspoon
    • Cumin powder: 1-2 teaspoon
    • Turmeric powder: 1/2 teaspoon
    • Red chilli: 1/2-1 teaspoon
    • Garam Masala: 1/2 teaspoon

    For Condiments

    • Butter: White or Salted
    • Plain Curd or Raita
    • Fresh Chutey: Mint, Tomato or as per liking
    • Pickles: as per liking

Steps to make Parantha

  1. Take the flour and mix salt, oil and knead it by adding water little by little.
  2. Once the dough is ready keep it aside in a airtight box or covered by cloth
  3. Take the filling material of your choice and mash it with your hands by adding the spices together. Make sure the filling is not lumpy or it will come out during rolling.
  4. Take a small ball sized dough or a little less and dust it with flour. flatten it and roll to a 3-4 inch roti.
  5. Add the filling of the same size or 80-90% of the dough size
  6. Press the stuffing evenly in the roti and close it from all sides with hands by pushing the filling inside.
  7. Once the filling is filled inside the dough. Dust the ball again with flour
  8. Flatten it with rolling pin with a gentle pressure. Roll it evenly by starting from middle to up and clockwise motion
  9. Prepare the tawa on high flame and grease it with 1/2 teaspoon ghee
  10. When the tawa is hot and ready, put the flame in medium and put the rolled parantha in it. Make sure to dust off excess flour.
  11. When you see bubbles forming on the surface turn it to other side.
  12. Spread some ghee over parantha's cooked side. Gently press down with spatula.
  13. When you see parantha is starting to puff, turn it again and put the ghee on it.
  14. Once both side are evely cooked and turned golden brown, remove it to the plate.
  15. Repeat the same step to make the roti and frying the parantha.
  16. Serve the parantha with your choice of condiments
  17. Enjoy!
